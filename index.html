Information and communications technology(ICT)

is an extensional term for information technology (IT) that stresses the role of unified communications
and the integration of telecommunications (telephone lines and wireless signals) and computers,
as well as necessary enterprise software, middleware, storage and audiovisual, that enable users
to access, store, transmit, understand and manipulate information

Digital technologies are pivotal in modern society, influencing nearly every aspect of our lives.
From communication and education to business operations and healthcare, their importance cannot be overstated. They enable unprecedented connectivity, efficiency, and innovation, driving progress and shaping the way we interact with the world. As technology continues to evolve, embracing and harnessing digital advancements becomes increasingly crucial for staying competitive, driving economic growth, and addressing global challenges.

Economics overview

Let's explore the evolution of the ICT sector within European economies over the last decade. Which countries have successfully integrated this sector deeply into their economies, and which ones lag behind, relying more on traditional methods rather than keeping pace with modern technological advancements?

Percentage of the ICT sector in GDP

In the graph on the left, distinct groups of countries emerge. The first cluster, comprising Bulgaria, Estonia, and Latvia, exhibits rapid growth in the ICT sector's share within the overall GDP. The second group , depicted in purple and consisting of Germany, Denmark, and Belgium, maintains a steady economy with a consistent average allocation of ICT resources compared to other countries. Lastly, the third cluster, highlighted in yellow and encompassing Malta and Finland, undergoes significant transformations, albeit with an overall positive trend.

ICT in Enterprises

In this section, we delve into the indicators of digitalization within enterprises: the extent of IT integration in business operations, the employment of ICT specialists, and their demographics

ICT personnel as part of total employment

Every technology relies on specialized support. As the ICT sector continues to expand, the average workforce in this field is also growing, as evidenced by the adjacent boxplot that depicts statistics of percentages of ICT specialists in total number of employees. We see that in countries experiencing particularly rapid growth (highlighted in red), the proportion of employees in this sector surpasses the European median.

Digital intensity in enterprises

The Digital Intensity Index (DII) serves as a composite indicator, formulated from surveys on ICT usage and e-commerce within enterprises. Within the framework of the Digital Decade, the DII stands as a critical performance metric. The adjacent divergent bar chart illustrates the DII across European countries, ranging from very low to very high. It's notable that a high ICT sector contribution to GDP does not invariably translate to a high DII, as evidenced by Bulgaria, for instance where despite the high sector of the industry, digital technologies are not included in most businesses.

ICT specialists' portrait

As depicted in the piechart, the average ICT specialist, regardless of their country of origin, tends to be a male aged over 35 with more than 5 years of higher education. While it would be inaccurate to speculate on causality, the higher average age is likely linked to the longer duration of university education.

Usage of ICT technologies by population

Populations' basic digital skills

The ICT sector encompasses both producers and consumers. Let's now focus on the latter. On the European map, colors represent the percentage of a country's population possessing basic digital skills. Notably, there appears to be no correlation with the previously examined groups. It appears that ICT products can have an international reach, suggesting that there's no strict requirement for demand within one's own country to foster related business development.

Gender Gap in digital skills

Now, let's delve into the gender disparities within the population. In the Dumbell plot, countries are arranged based on the magnitude of this gap: on the left are nations where a notable disparity exists between the digital skills level of men and women (for instance, in Austria, 68% of men possess such skills compared to 60% of women), while on the right are countries where a higher proportion of women possess digital skills.
